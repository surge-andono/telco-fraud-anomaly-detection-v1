# 🕵️ Fraud Case Studies: XL Axiata Context (2017-2022) 

    location at src/traffic_simulator.py

This document details the specific fraud scenarios simulated and detected in this project.

### 1. Simbox Fraud (Interconnect Bypass)
**Context**: Dominant during the 3G-4G transition (2017-2019) when interconnection rates were high.
* **Scenario**: Fraudsters use SIM boxes (hardware that holds hundreds of SIM cards) to terminate international VOIP calls into the local network. 
* **Detection Patterns**: 
    - High `call_to_dest_ratio` (Each call goes to a unique number).
    - Extremely short `avg_call_duration` (Signaling tests or failed handshakes).
* **Impact**: Loss of International Termination Revenue.

### 2. Data Abuse (Professional Tethering/Server)
**Context**: Rose with the popularity of "Unlimited" and "Large Volume" 4G data packages (2020-2022).
* **Scenario**: Commercial users (e.g., illegal ISPs or bot farms) use mobile SIMs for 24/7 server activities, violating Fair Usage Policies (FUP).
* **Detection Patterns**: 
    - Consistent `data_usage_intensity` (Stable traffic 24/7, unlike human behavior).
    - `daily_data_gb` exceeding the 99th percentile of normal users.
* **Impact**: Network congestion and reduced Quality of Service (QoS) for legitimate customers.
